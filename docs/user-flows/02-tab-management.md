# Working with Tabs

## Open a New Empty Tab

1. Click the **+** button at the top of the main area (next to your existing tabs).
2. A new blank tab appears called "Untitled" with default settings:
   - Method set to GET
   - Empty URL
   - No body, no auth, no captures
3. The new tab becomes your active tab.

## Switch Between Tabs

1. Click on any tab at the top of the screen.
2. The main area updates to show that tab's request and response.

## Close a Tab

1. Click the **×** button on the tab you want to close.
   - Or middle-click (scroll wheel click) on the tab.
2. If it's your only tab:
   - The tab closes and you see "No open requests" message.
3. If you have other tabs:
   - The tab closes and the nearest neighboring tab becomes active.

## Close All Tabs

1. Right-click on any tab.
2. Click **Close All Tabs** from the menu that appears.
3. All tabs are closed and you see the "No open requests" message.

## Duplicate a Tab

1. Right-click on a tab.
2. Click **Duplicate Request** from the menu.
3. An exact copy of that tab appears next to it, with:
   - All the same settings (URL, headers, body, etc.)
   - No response (you'll need to send the request again)
   - Not linked to any saved collection
4. The copy becomes your active tab.

## Drag to Reorder Tabs

1. Click and hold on a tab.
2. Drag it left or right to a new position.
3. Release to drop it in the new spot.
4. The tab order is saved automatically.

## Right-Click Menu on Tabs

1. Right-click on any tab.
2. A menu appears with three options:
   - **Duplicate Request** — makes a copy of the tab
   - **Close Tab** — closes that specific tab
   - **Close All Tabs** — closes every tab
3. Click anywhere else to dismiss the menu without choosing.

## Tab Names

- If you've given the request a name, that name appears on the tab.
- If you haven't named it but entered a URL, the URL path is shown.
- Otherwise, it shows "Untitled".

## Unsaved Changes Indicator

- When you edit a request (change URL, headers, body, etc.), a small dot (**●**) appears on the tab.
- This means you have unsaved changes.
- The dot disappears after you save the request to a collection.
